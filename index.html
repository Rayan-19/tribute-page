Albert Einstein

The man who changed a billion lives

Birth and Background:
  • Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Württemberg, Germany.
  • Secular, middle-class Jewish family.
  • Had one sister named Maria (Maja).
  • Father: Hermann Einstein (featherbed salesman and later an electrochemical factory owner).
  • Mother: Pauline Koch (ran the family household).
Early Fascination with Science:
  • Encountered a compass at age five, which sparked his interest in invisible forces.
  • Discovered a geometry book at age 12, which he called his “sacred little geometry book.”
Scientific Achievements:
  • Developed the special and general theories of relativity.
  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1921) for explaining the photoelectric effect.
  • Advocated against quantum theory randomness, famously saying, “God does not play dice.”
  • Attempted to devise a unified field theory combining gravitation and electromagnetism12.
  • Albert Einstein remains one of the most influential physicists of the 20th century!
